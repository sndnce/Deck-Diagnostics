WHAT IS SB-721?

Huntington Beach – Balcony inspection under SB-721 compliance

“The Balcony Law”

This statewide California law requires an inspection of “elevated exterior elements” for buildings with 3+ multifamily units.

  • Who: Multifamily (3+ units) with balconies, decks, stairs, catwalks.
  • When: First cycle due by January 1, 2026, then every 6 years.
  • Scope: Representative sample of EEEs; report filed per local requirements.

Penalty for Not Complying

If work isn’t completed within the mandated timeline (after a 30-day notice to cure), the governing agency may impose fines.

$100–$500
per unit, per day

What am I required to do?

  • If emergency repairs are required: Submit report to the authority within 15 days and begin work immediately.
  • If non-emergency repairs are noted: Obtain permit approval within 120 days of report receipt and submit a final report within 120 days of permit approval.

The SB-721 Compliance Process

  1. Set Up Review — Walk the property; confirm scope and units.
  2. Review Consultation — Discuss findings and cost ranges.
  3. Your Options — Plan funding & next steps if repairs are needed.
  4. Order Formal Inspection — Code-compliant EEE inspection & report.
  5. Repairs — Execute corrective work as required.
  6. Final Inspection — Close out and document compliance.
Potential results: Pass, Specific Damage (repairs recommended), or Emergency (immediate action required).
